The problem

Three reasons your plan
keeps overpaying

The biosimilar savings gap isn't a mystery. It's a conflict of interest — built into how your PBM makes money.

01

PBM rebate conflicts

Your PBM earns manufacturer rebates to keep brand drugs on formulary. A biosimilar switch eliminates that revenue. They have no incentive to surface the opportunity.

02

Broker knowledge gaps

Most benefits brokers lack the clinical knowledge to identify biosimilar switching opportunities — and their commission goes up when plan costs go up.

03

Nobody connecting the dots

Claims data, FDA approval status, interchangeability classifications, and benefit design changes all need to work together. No one is doing that analysis independently.
